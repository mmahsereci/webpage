**Early Stopping Without a Validation Set**
Early stopping is a widely used technique to prevent poor generalization performance when training an over-expressive model by means of gradient-based optimization. To find a good point to halt the optimizer, a common practice is to split the dataset into a training and a smaller validation set to obtain an ongoing estimate of the generalization performance. We propose a novel early stopping criterion based on fast-to-compute local statistics of the computed gradients and entirely removes the need for a held-out validation set.
*Maren Mahsereci, Lukas Balles, Christoph Lassner, Philipp Hennig* arXiv:1703.09580 preprint 2017.
