Active Multi-Information Source Bayesian Quadrature
The paper introduces active learning for Bayesian quadrature when multiple related information sources of variable cost in input and source are accessible. This setting arises for example when evaluating the integrand requires a complex simulation to be run that can be approximated by simulating at lower levels of sophistication and at lesser expense. We construct cost-sensitive multi-source acquisition rates and discuss pitfalls.
A. Gessner, J. Gonzalez, M. Mahsereci Proceedings of The 35th Uncertainty in Artificial Intelligence Conference (UAI) 2019, PMLR 115:712-721, 2020.
